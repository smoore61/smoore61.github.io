Sampling Explained

Sampling in dance music involves taking snippets of existing songs, sounds, or vocal tracks and incorporating them into new compositions. This technique allows producers to create fresh, innovative tracks while paying homage to the original material, often transforming familiar elements into something entirely unique. Sampling is a cornerstone of genres like house and hip-hop, driving the evolution of dance music through creative reimagining of sounds...

Basics of Scratching

Scratching as a DJ involves manipulating a vinyl record on a turntable to produce distinctive sounds and rhythms. To scratch, the DJ places their hand on the record and moves it back and forth while controlling the crossfader on the mixer to cut the sound in and out. Key techniques include the baby scratch, which is the basic forward and backward motion, and the transformer scratch, which involves rapid movements and precise fader control...
